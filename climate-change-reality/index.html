All words no action: Climate change is no longer a doomsday prophecy, it’s a reality!

Prime Minister Narendra Modi has called for a Global People’s Movement to bring about behavioural change to tackle “Climate Change”. Howdy Modi is over now. The Prime Minister should now attend some serious business and his words must match his actions towards steps taken to address climate change.

Just by addressing the matter for the sake of it will not help. Instead of urging the international community to tackle this, I would like to question my Prime Minister that what steps have his government taken to tackle this in India? Give me just five measures adopted by the Modi government which have been “fully implemented” in the past six years of his governance to address these problems. No, you can not take the country for granted and address these issues only in the United Nations and other international forums. In a way the Prime Minister was right when he said that “the time for talking is over, the world needs to act now”.

But is the government solely to blame? We as “responsible” citizens need to understand that we have to play a very pivotal role in combating this problem. At least we can keep our surroundings clean for a healthy livelihood. This itself will be a great achievement. We can not forget the calamities that happened in Uttarakhand, the poor air quality of our capital, the garbage thrown into the rivers. Namami Gange can not be a success unless we completely ban the activities on the river belt. The nature’s loss during the man made fire in Amazon was unprecedented and the loss was colossal. We are running the most dangerous experiment in history right now, which is to see how much carbon dioxide we can handle before there is an environmental catastrophe.

Photo: Reuters

The Climate Change Summit was inaugurated by a sixteen year old Swedish teenage, Greta Thunberg, climate change activist. With an angry outburst condemning the world leaders for failing to combat climate change she questioned “How Dare You?”. It was an emotional outburst punctuated with stern remarks on the generations that have polluted the most in the past and have burdened future generations with an extreme impact of climate change. The teenager was in a belligerent mood and rightly so because the present innocent ones have to fight a battle against climate hazards. Nature should be preserved and harnessed. There is no doubt that nature is angry today. We can not play with nature and think that we can fool it.

Quite recently the Modi government has banned the single use of plastic which is a great step taken to counter this, but is it enough? Renewable energy capacity has to be increased. PM Modi said that India has envisaged a coalition for disaster resilient infrastructure to help overcome the impact of disasters. Whatever planning has been done now needs to be implemented. Installation of solar panels on the roof tops will be of great help. Half measures will not work because we are really threatened by this problem globally. Corrective measures have to be adopted to pacify the mood of nature otherwise we should be prepared for more catastrophes.
