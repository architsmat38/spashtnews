Some good news coming from across the border in respect to Kartarpur Corridor

Photo: PTI

The Chief Minister of Punjab, Captain Amarinder Singh has extended an invitation to the former Prime Minister Manmohan Singh and present Prime Minister Narendra Modi to attend 550th birth anniversary of Guru Nanak, the founder of Sikhism next month. The event is expected to be inaugurated by the President of India, Shri Ram Nath Kovind. It was in Kartarpur (now in Pakistan) where the Sikh Guru spent the last few years of his life.

The CM is expected to walk across the Kartarpur corridor to Gurudwara Darbar Sahib in Pakistan. The date of Prime Minister Modi is yet to be finalised where as Prime Minister Manmohan Singh has agreed to join an all party delegation which will be heading across the border to Darbar Sahib. The Kartarpur corridor will be thrown open on the 9th of November. This will allow access to pilgrims from India to the Shrine which was established by Guru Nanak Devji in 1522. It was here that he passed away in the year 1539. Work on the four lane on the Indian side would be completed shortly where as Pakistan announced that work on its side would be completed in October.

The Kartarpur corridor on the Pakistan side is being built from the Indian border to Gurudwara Darbar Sahib while the other part of the corridor from Dera Baba Nanak upto the border is being constructed by India. Earlier the former Prime Minister Manmohan Singh declined an invite by the Pakistan government to attend the inauguration ceremony on its side but no invite was extended to Prime Minister Narendra Modi. Perhaps it was some sort of an ego satisfaction by the Pakistan government. In Spite of the escalated tensions between the two countries, it is good that finally the corridor will be opened to the Indian pilgrims.

It has been clarified by the Punjab CM that no one is going to attend the opening on the Pakistan side. Indian government has assured of full security to its pilgrims who would visit this corridor. It has been made clear by the Congress that the former PM will not accept the invite of Pakistan government. The former PM never visited Pakistan during his tenure as Prime Minister of India from 2004 - 2014. But would be a part of the “Jattha” which will be headed by the Punjab CM to visit the corridor post the inauguration.

Finally there is something to cheer about in the declining Indo Pak relations.
