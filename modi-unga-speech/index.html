PM Modi discusses development programs whereas PM Imran Khan propagates war threat

Photo: Reuters

Prime Minister Narendra Modi’s address to United Nations General Assembly (UNGA) was an ambitious one. The PM laid stress on various socio economic aspects and its development. It was made very clear at the very outset that he would not discuss Kashmir in Pakistan and would rather focus on the emergence of India as an upcoming powerhouse. This was a departure from his earlier addresses which were mainly restricted to Pakistan and Kashmir. The PM said that the world has to unite against terrorism and there can be no two opinions on this. The PM emphasised the accomplishments of sanitation and Ayushman Bharat and its successful implementations.

To quote the PM - “We belong to the country that has given Buddha and not war”. He further mentioned that for the sake of humanity the world must unite against terrorism. In a sharp contrast, the Pakistan Prime Minister Imran Khan once again torn into the Kashmir issue and warned of a “bloodbath” in Kashmir which would culminate into a nuclear warfare once the sanctions have been lifted in the Valley. He termed it as “illegal annexation”.

The address to the UNGA by the Indian PM was also special as we all are celebrating 150th birth anniversary of the Father of our nation Mahatma Gandhi whose message of non violence is being practised all over the globe. This was PM Mod’s third address to the UNGA and was met with plenty of dhols, cheers and fanfare on the streets. We witnessed a Diwali festive mood by the Indian community which was rare and unprecedented outside the country. The crowd was in a frenzy and danced to drums.

The PM did not disappoint the Indians and gave a crisp message to the world in respect to Climate change, Terrorism and Socio economic changes happening in India. He mentioned that India is running a serious campaign to ban the use of single use plastic. He also highlighted the other development goals of his government in respect to sanitation and health insurance for the economically weaker sections. He plans to eradicate Tuberculosis by 2025 and build 20 million more houses for the poor by 2022 when we celebrate the diamond jubilee of our Independence.

If all these are implemented within the stipulated time, we can make rapid strides in making our country a developed nation. PM Modi also promoted the message of Swami Vivekananda - “harmony, peace and no dissension”. He said that the message of the world’s largest democracy to the world community remained the same.
