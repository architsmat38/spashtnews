Fate of People in Assam Post NRC

The National Register of Citizens (NRC) is a register containing the names of all genuine citizens of India. It was first prepared in 1951. As of now, the NRC is being updated for the state of Assam. Ever since the updation of NRC was announced there has been a lot of tension and speculation. NRC is a record of all legal citizens in a state. The need of NRC in Assam was rising because the locals felt that the illegal foreigners were dominating their land. This led to the Assam Agitation in the 1980s. The Assam Gana Parishad and the All Assam Students’ Union submitted a memorandum to the Center seeking the updation in the list. The six year long agitation culminated with the signing of the Assam Accord on 15th August, 1985 in the presence of the then Prime Minister Rajiv Gandhi.

The idea behind this was to reflect the true population of Assam. This was to be ensured by updating all the names (or their descendants) on the electoral up till 1971. Even those names that were included in the initial NRC of 1951 had to be put in the updated NRC. Post the first draft that was published in January 2018, only 1.9 crore people were included in the list out of 3.9 crores who filed the NRC application. There was an overriding concern about the fates of delisted people and their rights. There was speculation that they might be confined in detention centers as them being deported to Bangladesh was ruled out. The final list was released today with around 19 Lakh exclusions. All those whose names have not met the final list will have to fight the battle in the foreigners tribunals to prove themselves as Indians.

The Assam Chief Minister, Sarbananda Sonowal asked the people not to panic and the necessary measures will be taken to provide legal assistance. The BJP’s wait and watch policy does not give a very clear picture of what will take place in Assam over the next few months. However, one this is for sure - the government wants to take things slowly. The recent Home Ministry notification said that “non-inclusion of a person’s name in the NRC does not by itself amount to him/her being declared a foreigner”. The extension of time limit to appeal before tribunals may vary from 60 to 120 days. Only a Foreign Tribunal (FT) will have the right to declare a person a foreigner and if he/she is dissatisfied, they can challenge the FT in High Court and then the Supreme Court.

NRC Final Assam List can be checked online at http://nrcassam.nic.in/
