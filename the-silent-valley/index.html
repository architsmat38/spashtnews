The Silent Valley

Photo: millichronicle

US President Donald Trump’s intervention in Kashmir is all over, after he agreed that it is a bilateral issue to be resolved by India and Pakistan. The two leaders were meeting for the first time after abrogation of Article 370. They also discussed many other bilateral issues ahead of India Prime Minister, Narendra Modi’s, visit to New York. Trump’s statement is a departure from the earlier statement he made, mentioning that he could mediate on the insistence of Prime Minister Modi. Trump feels that this can be solved bilaterally but Pakistan has its own take on this.

Even after being snubbed by the US President, Pakistan is not the one to sit quiet, specially with China’s assurance of it’s support and commitment. Pakistan Prime Minister, Imran Khan, described the scrapping of Article 370 as a historic blunder for which India would have to pay heavily in the coming months. There has been an escalation in deployment of troops by Pakistan across the Line of Control. Imran Khan has been continuously threatening of consequences which may lead to a nuclear war amongst the two nations. Their Army Chief, General Qamar Javed Bajwa, is creating a situation across border that may instigate violence. It may eventually lead to a war like Kargil. Pakistan has launched a vicious maligned information campaign to cause unrest. As of now all the attempts have been proven to be abortive.

Kashmir is definitely not addressed. The situation is not at all normal with no cellular network. All the opposition leaders have been denied access to any information and were barred from entering the valley. The Indian Army considers saving the life of the innocent as its priority. But we should not undermine the deployment of troops along the border area. Many infiltration attempts by the terrorists have been thwarted by the Indian Army. But the eerie silence is very loud indicating of a backlash by the goons of Pakistan Army. India has made its stand very clear internationally, that New Delhi will not consider Kashmir as an issue to be discussed with Islamabad, because it no longer exists to be an issue. But as of now, the Valley is at unrest and the coming months are going to be very crucial. It is here that a strong Indian government headed by Prime Minister Modi will have to call the shorts in Kashmir and bring parity to the Valley without any foreign intervention.
