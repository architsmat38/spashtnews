The Mahatma of the Nation : Gandhiji’s two weapons, Satyagraha and Non Violence

Photo: PTI

The entire nation is celebrating the 150th birth anniversary of the greatest Indian ever, Mohandas Karamchand Gandhi, the Father of our Nation. Gandhiji was a visionary and led the Indian Freedom Struggle with two main weapons of Satyagraha and Non Violence, which inspired many political leaders and mass movements in many countries. We are into the 72nd year of our Independence and young enough to remember the impact of Gandhiji in the struggle for freedom.

A whole new generation after Gandhiji passed away came into existence and should be made aware that why Gandhiji matter even today. In Fact the truth is he matters to us more than ever before. It is very difficult to concise him in an article. He never believed in violence. Gandhiji revealed how socio economic barriers could be broken with the use of non violence. His views will always be a reminder to the downtrodden, the oppressed that greatness can be achieved without resorting to violence. Mahatma Gandhi’s biographer, Ram Chander Guha has mentioned that traces of Gandhi’s life are spread across the world from South Africa to Israel, from the United Kingdom to the United States. The archives in Mahatma’s homeland too are a treasure trove of information. Gandhiji has been indispensable to the nation in the past and even now when he is not with us. With so much of violence spread across the globe we really need to revisit his practice of non violence.

Photo: Wikipedia

MK Gandhi always stood for social equality and fought for the oppressed of the society and translated his vision into action, which we all in present time need to inculcate in our lives. He laid the foundation of Satyagraha and Non Violence, and centered all his political movements around these two practises. He was a sacred soul and was all ready to sacrifice himself for principles and ideologies. To quote Albert Einstein - “Generations to come, it may well be will believe that such a man as this one, ever in flesh and blood tread upon this Earth”. Gandhiji’s life is a strong message for the entire nation.

The best tribute which can pay him on his 150th birth anniversary is to take a pledge to emulate his dedicated and transparent life adapt renunciation in our lives to make Bharat as a teacher to the entire world. If we inculcate little of what he practiced we would have done good not only for ourselves but for the generations to follow.
