The "Anands" of Chinmayanand : Such Swamis are a disgrace to the society

Photo: PTI

Finally Swami Chinmayanand has been arrested after the victim threatened to kill herself if the Special Investigation Team (SIT) did not arrest the accused. The BJP leader is a rape accused of a law student and was arrested today by the SIT from his Ashram. The SIT also arrested three more with charges of extortion from the former Union Minister and the accused Swami. Chinmayanand has confessed and has apologised to the police for his behaviour. He has admitted that he used to call the woman for a body massage. This has been recorded and viewed during investigation.

But there is a conflict between the Uttar Pradesh Police and the SIT on the charges to be levied against him. Article 376 of the Indian Penal Code refers to rape but the SIT has issued a lesser charge as listed in Article 376C. Article 376 states that the accused may have to spend the rest of his life in prison whereas Article 376C relaxes the punishment between five to ten years. The victim was a law student in the college run by the seventy-two year old Swami.

Who is Swami Chinmayanand?

Born as Krishna Pal Singh, he was a former Union Minister from Uttar Pradesh. He was a Minister of State in the Vajpayee government and represented Jaunpur constituency in the year 1999. This act of Chinmayanand is a slur on the BJP government because it has yet to recover from the accusations of Kuldeep Singh Sengar, fourth term MLA in the Yogi government.

Chinmayanand has also been accused of stalking and wrongful confinement. The woman claims that he showed her a video of her bathing, threatened to make it viral and then blackmailed her. The student has alleged that she was repeatedly raped by the Swami for the past one year. She has a pen drive and a video recording as evidence. The woman was brave enough to record all the incidences by ordering a pair of special glasses from Flipkart. This eventually led to a series of allegations and counter allegations. The law student is not satisfied with the charges faced against the Swami (Article 376C) as it will weaken the case. Maybe an escape route is being planned. Last year, the Uttar Pradesh government has tried to withdraw a 2011 rape case against Chinamayanand, but its petition was rejected by a court in Shahjahanpur. The video clip clearly shows the victim giving a massage to the Swami as shown by the SIT. The Swami was quick to accept the crime committed by him. After conducting a medical examination he was produced in court and then sent to jail.

Where are we heading with our moral values?

They are at its lowest ebb. It is a shame that such shenanigans are being carried by our politicians whom we elect to protect our rights. Many leaders have been accused of rape in the past and this is a far cry from the slogan “Beti Padhao Beti Bachao”. As of now, I think it is all about Beti Bachao.
