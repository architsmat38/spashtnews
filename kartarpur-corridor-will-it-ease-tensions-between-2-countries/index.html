Kartarpur Corridor - Will it ease tensions between the two countries or will it be a passage for separatist activities in Punjab?

Photo: Twitter, @ImranKhanPTI

The Kartarpur corridor would be inaugurated by the Prime Minister Narendra Modi on Saturday, 9th November. India is quite aware of the Pakistanâ€™s motive on Kartarpur corridor. Pakistan military has envisaged a certain purpose in opening of the corridor and that is to increase Sikh separatism in Punjab. However, the Indian government is still committed on inaugurating the corridor for the Sikh pilgrims for whom it is considered to be the most revered shrine. There is a duality in the corridor and India is aware of the ulterior motives of Pakistan. India is keeping its eyes open but as a democratic government it fully respects the feelings of the Sikhs.

India had earlier objected to the photographs of former Sikh separatist leader, Jarnail Singh Bhindranwala and the other terrorists in the 1980s, as well as pictures of posters calling for a referendum “2020” in a promotional video released by the Pakistan media. The Indian government has raised its concerns with the Pakistan government over the threat to Indian dignitaries who will visit the corridor on Saturday. Security threat looms large on former Prime Minister Manmohan Singh and Punjab Chief Minister Captain Amarinder Singh by the Sikh separatist groups such as Pakistan based Babbar Khalsa and Khalistan Liberation Force.

Pakistan TV channels have been reporting that Pakistan Prime Minister Imran Khan has exempted all pilgrims travelling for the inauguration from passports to only identification. However, the protocol between the two countries indicate that passport is the only official document at present. With just two days left for the inauguration of the corridor, no advance team from India has been given permission to look over the arrangements at the Kartarpur Gurudwara site. Lack of bonhomie between the officials of the two countries just ahead of the opening of the corridor is a big deterrent in establishing peace and calmness.

Former Prime Minister Manmohan Singh hoped that the opening of the corridor will help ease things between the two nations in the future. He said that peace and harmony is the only way forward for a prosperous future. He appealed to one and all in a special session of the Punjab Assembly to commemorate the 550th birth anniversary of Guru Nanak Dev ji along with Vice President Venkaiah Naidu. The VP described Guru Nanak Dev ji as one of Indiaâ€™s most democratic spiritual leaders. He said that his vision has as much relevance today as it was when expounded five centuries ago. The session was also attended by Haryana Chief Minister Manohar Lal Khattar, Deputy Chief Minister Dushyant Chautala, Punjab and Haryana Governors.

But whatever the outcome of the inauguration of Kartarpur corridor, we all hope and pray that peace and tranquility is maintained by both the countries on such a pious occasion. This would be a true homage to Guru Nanak Dev ji on his 550th birth anniversary.
