Reviving Indian Economy

The increase in Gross Domestic Product (GDP) is a parameter to measure the country’s economic growth. The GDP is defined as the combined value of all goods and services produced in a country annually. There is no single factor that influences the economy of a country. Consistency in the growth is the key to uplift the economy of the country. Recessions do occur and could be caused due to multiple geo-political and geo-financial factors. These factors affecting the economy have been debated several times by the world leaders and economists to achieve an ideal growth rate. We need to understand the measures that take an economy forward on a wider perspective.

For instance, the United States economy is driven by consumer spending and investment in businesses. This holds true for India as well. The well functioning of real estate gives an enormous rise to the GDP of the country. If the consumers are buying homes, the benefits are passed onto the contractors and construction workers, thus resulting in an economic growth. Businesses have a pivotal role to play in increasing the growth of an economy because they help in creating jobs. Banks also play an integral part in taking the economy forward with their lending policies to the business houses. Businessmen explore new ideas with enough financial support from the banks. Business investments and consumer spending help a great deal for the country’s economy to grow. The tax policy of a country influences consumer spending with the help of rebates and easing of taxes. This helps in flow of cash in the market. The two main factors - consumer spending and business investments have hit an all time low in our country resulting in the low GDP of 5% in the last quarter. Cash flow has decreased and the consumer is hardly left with any cash for spending.

The above mentioned factors must increase for economic growth. Tax rebates should help in stimulating the Indian economy. We can relax the regulations imposed on the industry, like happened in the US in the 1980s when the US government deregulated several industries. India needs a robust economic growth very soon to bring it back on the wheels of progress. Bank mergers are a big leap forward to correct the economic mess. But it is still too early to draw a conclusion.

Former Prime Minister Manmohan Singh had urged the Central government to take advice from the sane voices so that the lost economy can be regained. He has given an in depth remedy for a sagging economy. Rationalisation of GST, reviving the agricultural sector, addressing liquidity crisis, reviving major job generating sectors like automobiles, textiles and exploring new markets that are opening up due to the ongoing trade war between the US and China are the 5 points that the Former Prime Minister suggests which require attention. These are very methodological measures that if adapted will give a strong impetus to the growth of Indian economy.
