Worrying state of the Indian economy at 5% GDP growth

Indian economy is heading towards a winter recession in a state of trepidation. Anybody who thinks that we are not facing a serious economic crisis is turning a blind eye. Earlier, SpashtNews had made it very clear that the country was heading towards a depressed economic situation. Our GDP growth has hit a low of 5% since March 2013. The previous low was 4.9% in quarter 1 of 2012-2013. Although the Finance Minister, Nirmala Sitharaman has announced a plethora of measures to boost the economy last month, seems like she has lost all clues to revive the fast dipping economy. Will these measures result in the upliftment of the economy is a matter of conjecture.

The government in its measures has announced to augment business sentiment and to boost consumption demand in real estate and automobile sector which are going to be one of the worst crises of recent times. The government in its efforts has scrapped attacks on foreign portfolio to improve the business sentiments of the country. The government is trying to increase credit supply through bank loans and through non banking financial companies. There has been a steep fall in consumption. The handling of this crisis has come for severe criticism by the opposition headed by Congress. Since Demonetisation and a hurried GST, corporate business has been on the decline in the country. Manufacturing and agricultural growths have shown no sign of redemption. The stock market which was doing exceptionally well till the month of July’19 is also on the decline with the Sensex coming down practically everyday. The private consumption demand has been hit badly and there is hardly any movement in real estate and automobile industry which caters to a high consumption. This is a very grave situation for any country to be in.

The former Prime Minister (PM) and one of the greatest economists, Manmohan Singh has expressed his serious concern over the depleting condition of the Indian economy. GDP growth plummeting to a low of 5% is itself an indicator that we are caught in a deep cauldron of economic crisis. The former PM stated that the country has not recovered since the two major blunders Demonetisation and GST. He has also tweeted and has urged the Modi government to reach out to all sane voices to steer the economy out of this crisis. The government should come forward with a blueprint oh how it is planning to budget its 1.76 lakh crore help from the RBI to put some wind in the Indian economy. There is much more needed from the government to boost the potential growth.
