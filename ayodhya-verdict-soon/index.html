Ayodhya Verdict soon, Security beefed up in vulnerable areas. May peace prevail in the land of Lord Rama

Photo: trak.in

Security has been beefed up in Ayodhya ahead of long awaited Supreme Court verdict on litigated Ram Mandir and Babri Masjid issue. About 4000 security forces have been deployed in Ayodhya to avoid any mishap. With the verdict about to be announced in a few days the Ministry of Home Affairs have also issued an advisory to all states to maintain law and order.

The Court has reserved judgement and a verdict is expected anytime before November 17th when the Chief Justice of India, Ranjan Gogoi, will retire. A seven page advisory has been sent to all the Zonal headquarters to maintain security at places of worship. The Railway Protection Force (RPF) have identified 80 sensitive stations and asked its personnel to keep a tight vigil. Uttar Pradesh Chief Minister, Yogi Adityanath, held a video conferencing with Police Officers in charge of all districts and reviewed security to tackle any eventuality. Uttar Pradesh DGP OP Singh has increased patrolling in the vulnerable areas that have identified by it. A riot controlled team has also been prepared by all district police officials.

Ayodhya is anxious, and will remain so till the verdict has not been announced. Ahead of the verdict, police are vetting social media accounts to spot trouble mongers. Senior Superintendent of Police, Ajay Sahni (Meerut) has asked the cyber cell team in the district to look into the accounts of those making inflammatory comments. In the past 24 hours ten such accounts have been traced. One of the most prominent faces of the legal case was Hashim Ansari who was known for propagating a peaceful solution and one of the last persons to have personal knowledge of this case from the early years of Independence. He died in 2016 at a ripe age of 95. The baton was then passed on to the Junior Ansari and even he like his father propagated peace at any cost. “In 70 years we did whatever we could,” said Iqbal.

Those who believe in the Constitution will obey the Court. Most of the Ayodhya residents irrespective of the religious identity blame the outsiders for instigating the atmosphere in Ayodhya in the past. The Ganga-Jamuni tehzeeb of its people will not be affected by the verdict if such people are kept out. In fact, any curfew in the region affects both Hindus and the Muslims because if there are no pilgrims, it is a major setback for both the communities. Many in Ayodhya also want an end to the stalemate because now it is becoming a real pain for people of both religions. A certain fear has crept in the entire region and everyone is anxious for the verdict (that is to happen soon) so that people can live in peace and harmony.
