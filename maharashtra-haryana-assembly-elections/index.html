Maharashtra and Haryana may influence the fate of the depleted Opposition?

Photo: PTI

A major dip was registered in the Assembly Elections held in the two states of Haryana and Maharashtra. The total turnout was 60% in Maharashtra and 65% in Haryana compared to 63% and 76% respectively in 2014. The turnout was low in urban areas in Maharashtra compared to rural areas, whereas Faridabad and Gurgaon in Haryana registered the least turnout.

At a press conference at the Election Commission of India (ECI) headquarters, the Deputy Election Commissioners Chandrabhushan and Sandeep Saxena said that they witnessed a low turnout in both the states as compared to 2014. By-election was also held in 51 Assembly seats across the country and two Lok Sabha constituencies of Samastipur in Bihar and Sitara in Maharashtra. Polling altogether was smooth barring a few sporadic incidents. ECI added that a record number of alcohol was seized from both the states, which is a usual phenomenon during elections. Cash, narcotics, precious metals and other freebies were seized in Haryana and Maharashtra.

Polling across Haryana was by and large peaceful barring micro incidents in Rohtak and Narnaul districts. The elections were once again fought on the “Hindutva” symbol which has now become synonymous with BJP and elections anywhere in the country. Kashmir and Pakistan along with Article 370 was the main “Mudda” on which the BJP focussed its entire election campaign which was headed by the Prime Minister Narendra Modi and Home Minister Amit Shah. “Vikas” has taken a back seat on strong Hindu sentiments.

All the exit polls have single handedly given a huge mandate to the National Democratic Alliance (NDA) in both the states. The figures may touch two-thirds in Maharashtra and three-fourth in Haryana which once again projected a dismal performance by the opposition, mainly Congress. If the exit polls are to be believed and if they turn out to be exact then God save the Opposition. This may prove to be a major dent for Congress post Lok Sabha polls debacle. But the exit polls may not always be correct. It may not be a cakewalk for BJP, Shiv Sena combined as they may face stiff resistance for Congress NCP alliance. Same holds true for Haryana also.

The results on 24th October maybe a curtain raiser for things to happen in Delhi Assembly Elections because then a depleted opposition (Congress) might face a huge shift of its leaders to either Aam Aadmi Party or BJP. Congress needs to pull its socks up to make some impact as a strong opposition otherwise it will always be a cakewalk for the ruling party in all forthcoming elections.
