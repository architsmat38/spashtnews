The Economic Slowdown - How well is India prepared?

For the time being, Kashmir and Article 370 can take a back seat. There is a more serious business to be taken care of. A heavy debate is going on in the country about one of the worst economic slow-down in probably more than four decades. Post the landslide victory of the BJP led by Prime Minister Narendra Modi in the Lok Sabha polls, Indian stock market was bullish and the Rupee on the spurt. But even the Prime Minister knew that once the euphoria subsided, some tough economic challenges would be a big hurdle in making India a developed nation from a developing nation.

We need to understand that we witnessed some bold reforms from the government in the first term, such as the Bankruptcy Law to tackle Non Performing Assets (NPAs). India rose to a high of 77th position in the World Bank’s business ranking and became one of the fastest growing economy during that period. But then Demonetization backfired which gave a major setback to the economic development. The roll out of GST was a hurried affair with many glitches and did not go well with the common man. Prime Minister Modi had won the second term on all these laurels and a weak opposition. Modi government may take some bold decisions in the coming five years on the back of such a heavy mandate. But the surge in the economic slowdown is getting more evident.

The Gross Domestic Product (GDP) growth rate is being projected at 6.2%, with the Automobile Industry being one of the worst affected in the recent times. Showrooms are being shut down with huge inventory being unsold, jobs are being lost due to decline in vehicle sales. 3000 temporary jobs in Maruti were cut due to the economic slowdown. To top it, Finance Minister Nirmala Sitharaman presented such a budget that dented investor confidence due to some very stringent tax proposals. The business mood in India is very subdued. There is hardly any movement in the corporate sector. The government needs to take a quick understanding of this situation, otherwise it may get from bad to worse. Recently, the Reserve Bank of India (RBI) has come forward to influx some life in a depressed economy and announced a transfer of Rs 1.76 lakh crores to the Government of India during this financial year. This has come with severe criticism and scathing attack from Congress Ex President, Rahul Gandhi who was very vociferous that the Prime Minister and the Finance Minister have taken the Indian economy to the brink of disaster. He mentioned that this is like stealing money from the RBI. Probably the former RBI Governor Urjit Patel and the Deputy Governor Viral Acharya could visualize the slowdown in economy and henceforth resigned. Maybe their advice was falling on deaf ears.

In my view, RBI has tried to bail out the Center from a Catch-22 situation. The solution to this economic slowdown is difficult even with this mammoth help from the RBI. The government will have to take steps soon to come out of this situation. The only immediate solution for India seems uplifting the consumption through a stimulus given directly to people. Only Prime MInister Modi can take India out of this economic mess.
