Mission Moon: Chandrayaan 2

My memory takes me back to the famous lines that were once recited by the former Prime Minister and one of the most dynamic Prime Ministers ever, Shri Atal Bihari Vajpayee that read -

क्‍या हार में क्‍या जीत में

किंचित नहीं भयभीत मैं

संधर्ष पथ पर जो मिले यह भी सही वह भी सही।

वरदान माँगूँगा नहीं।।

Hats off to The India Space and Research Organisation (ISRO) and its team of scientists led by K Sevan for nearly pulling out a miracle they had dreamt of. The entire team deserves a big pat on the back for their yeoman effort in making India land on the moon. I will not discuss failures for they are the pillars of success.

The last fifteen minutes were torturous for the ISRO scientists who had sweated it out to realise the dreams of crores of countrymen. India was right there at the cusp of glory but this was not to be and the drooping shoulders with moist eyes said it all. It was great to have Prime Minister Narendra Modi at the ISRO centre to witness history and make our country proud. The way ISRO Chief broke down in the arms of PM Modi, it brought tears to all the countrymen who were wide awake to witness one of the most glorious moments of Bharat. It indeed was very poignant the way PM Modi was consoling one and all and helping them regain their composure. Had this happened, the country would have been bracketed in the elite club of nations to have achieved this after the US, Russia and China. History was in the making but we will have to wait a little long for this to happen. This will not deter our scientists to gather their bearings and try again. Chandrayaan 2 was a mission which was being watched by the entire world and no doubt even they would have been watching this with bated breath. The orbital is still there for a year and would be transmitting information to the ISRO scientists. This is also no mean achievement. The ISRO scientists will definitely be successful the second time and there is no doubt about it.

Quoting what the first climber of Mount Everest, Edmund Hillary had to say after his first attempt was abortive in scaling the highest peak - “I will come again and conquer you, because as a mountain you can not grow but as a human I can”. One day the Indian Tricolour will definitely hoist on moon.
