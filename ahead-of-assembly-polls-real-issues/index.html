Ahead of Assembly Polls let’s discuss the REAL ISSUES instead of mudslinging the Opposition

Photo: PTI, Outlook, Reuters

The national interest is once again at the forefront of the election campaign being carried out by the Prime Minister Narendra Modi. In yet another sharp attack on the Opposition combining the Congress and Nationalist Congress Party (NCP), the Prime Minister accused them of speaking against the national interest.

Addressing a rally in Maharashtra’s Jaina district for the 21st October State Assembly polls PM Modi said that the Congress was taking its last breath and sees ‘Rashtrabhakti’ in ‘Parivarbhakti’. He also criticized alliance partner NCP for its misdoings by referring to the summons of Enforcement Directorate (ED) to senior NCP leader Praful Patel in connection to money laundering investigation. However, Patel has denied any such involvement and has made himself available for any questioning.

PM Modi was in for a scathing attack by the opposition and rightly so because if anyone in general and political parties in particular questions the policies of the Modi Government, they have been termed as ‘anti-nationals’. PM Modi might think himself to be immortal on the political front but he really needs to understand that it is high time he starts addressing the real issues of the country.

Farmer suicides in the State have increased particularly in Marathwara and Vedharbha region. But once again the BJP has started making a fool of the electorate by promising to generate one crore jobs in the state but still has to fulfil the promise of 2014 to generate two crore jobs. Instead, there has been a big downfall in the employment sector which is at its lowest in the past 45 years. PM Modi, it seems has nothing to talk about except anti nationalism issues of the Opposition or raise the sentiments of Indo Pak rhetoric. Nothing, virtually nothing is happening and adapting measures to improve the depleting economy. Former Maharashtra Chief Minister and Senior Congress leader Ashok Chauhan has alleged that the BJP led government is targeting and poaching leaders from the Opposition ahead of the Assembly polls.

Why was PM Modi chosen? Only to discuss Indo Pak and Ram Mandir issues?

The PM must not take the country for a ride. He needs to get the economy back on track, generate jobs, increase consumer needs and demands in the country. The real estate and automobile sector is bleeding, farmers are committing suicides and the Indian youth is jobless. These are the issues which the PM needs to address rather than blow his own trumpet of anti nationalism. Questioning the government is not anti nationalism, instead it is a sign that the people of the country want to be involved in the decision making process. The people are getting tired of his idiosyncrasies and there must be a stop to this, otherwise he may face the same fate of Congress in the coming years. The Modi government definitely needs to mend its ways.
