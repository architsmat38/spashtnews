Revisiting the Ayodhya Dispute

Photo: Outlookindia

The Constitution bench has heard the case for forty days and is the Supreme Court’s second longest oral hearing in its history. The longest being the Kesavananda Bharati case lasted for 68 days.

The belief that Lord Rama was born in Treta Yuga 9,00,000 years ago where now stands the demolished Babri Masjid is the core dispute. The Masjid was built by Mir Baqi on the commands of Mughal Emperor Babur in the sixteenth century after having demolished a temple there. The Masjid was built in approximately 1500 sq yards and was demolished by the kar sevaks in December 1992.

Let’s have a look at the timelines of this case -

1949 - the idols of Ram Lalla (infant Ram) were placed inside the central dome of the mosque and the next year in 1950, Gopal Simla Visharad filed a suit in Faizabad court for the rights to worship the idols of Ram Lalla. The then Prime Minister Jawahar Lal Nehru spoke with the then Chief Minister GB Pant that a dangerous example is being set here and the issue must be addressed. However the request was declined.

1959 - Nirmohi Akhara (a religious denomination and one of the fourteen akharas of the Akhil Bharatiya Akhara Parishad) filed a case for the possession of the site.

1981 - The Uttar Pradesh Sunni Central Waqf Board filed a suit for declaration and possession of the site.

1986 - The Local Court ordered the government to open site for Hindu worshippers.

1989 - Another case filed in the name of Ram Lalla Virajman for declaration and possession.

1991 - UP Government acquired land for convenience of devotees coming for Darshan.

1992 - Babri Masjid demolished in a political rally by kar sevaks.

1993 - Centre took some land around the area and asked for the Supreme Court’s opinion regarding the case. The following year litigation reached the Lucknow Bench.

1996 - 2010 - Suits were heard.

Finally on 30th September 2010, the Allahabad High Court decided that the land be equally distributed between the Hindu MahaSabha, Sunni Waqf Board and Nirmohi Akhara. Also the court agreed that there existed a temple or temple structure before the mosque was built. In 2016, BJP leader Subramaniam Swamy filed a plea seeking construction of Ram Temple. The hearing was initiated again in September. The Supreme Court finally asked the parties to finish their arguments latest by the 18th of October and the judgement will be pronounced by 17th November when Chief Justice of India, Rajan Gogoi will retire.

Photo: PTI

Political Angle

The piece of land has always been a political agenda and has defined and redefined politics several times. More than 25 years have passed by and this has left a serious impact on the socio-political fabric of India. A lot of political analysts believe that the Ayodhya Case seems to be a political issue rather than a religious one.

We need to accept and respect the fact that India is a country with diverse faiths, cultures and languages. It’s in the developing stage where the entire nation should be more worried about growth and development of the country rather than being glued to their television sets wondering who is going to win a piece of land. How come no political party ever thought of building a school or a hospital on the disputed land? It could have benefitted people from every religion. Would that not be a true “Ram Rajya”? This reminds me of a few lines I read a few days ago -

On one hand we talk about Digital India while on the other we are still trying to figure out the exact room where Lord Rama was born 9,00,000 years ago. But of course, we live in times where Vote Bank politics is more important than National Integration.
