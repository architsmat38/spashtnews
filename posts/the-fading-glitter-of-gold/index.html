The Fading Glitter of Gold

All that glitters is not gold NOW for a common man. The yellow metal prices continue to rise further touching a 40,000 mark today.

How does gold affect the economy?

Investors generally buy gold as a protection either from an economic crisis or inflation. Low gold prices indicate a healthy economy. People invest in gold and consider it as a direct investment. Reminding me of the Great Depression (1929-1939), the price of gold went from $20.67 an ounce to $35 an ounce in 1934. The Federal Reserve was trying to maintain the gold standard which in turn helped the cause.

Why is this happening in the first place?

If the trade wars continue to escalate we might be inching towards a global recession. The US treasury yield curve, inverted for the first time since 2007. Economies of China and Germany have been ignoble as well. China had the weakest industrial output growth in the last 17 years while German economy slumped in the second quarter. All of this increase the fear of global recession. The US Federal Reserve decided to lower the interest rates which also benefitted the gold prices.

How will India be affected?

India is the world’s second biggest consumer of gold. Since the GDP is on decline, the production of goods and services are on decline too. Gold is generally denominated in dollars. Depreciating Indian rupee increases the imported price of gold in India. Unfortunately, the trend is going to continue for gold as well as silver. In a situation like this, as per the former Chairman of All India Gems and Jewellery Federation, Bachhraj Bamalwa, the fresh purchases will decrease and people will opt for recycling the old gold. However, he also mentioned that the demand will be marginally affected as the festive and wedding season is going to begin sometime soon in October. The season is incomplete without gold purchase in India. There is no stopping the Indians from buying gold no matter how steep the escalation in prices are. But with this depressed economy the gold is fast losing it’s glitter.
