Collision in Coalition

Photo: AFP

Amidst criticism from the Opposition, Governor of Maharashtra BS Koshiyari clamped President’s rule in the State when it became clear that no party was in a position to form a government, thus putting to an end nearly three weeks of political instability. But indications are such that if any party was in a position to form a government, the President Rule could be revoked.

Shiv Sena, ally of BJP which has fallen out has knocked the doors of Supreme Court blaming the Governor of working at the behest of BJP. The petition was filed on Wednesday challenging the decision. The BJP and Shiv Sena had earlier failed to form a government together. The Shiv Sena has been in touch with NCP leader Sharad Pawar and Congress leader Sonia Gandhi but even they need time to ponder and sign a common minimum program in order to form a stable government. The Congress, NCP and Shiv Sena indicated that they were open to discussions on forming a government once they reached an understanding on common minimum program.

Sena chief, Udhav Thackarey spoke in favour of joining hands with NCP and Congress and said that “if politics is heading in a new direction then we should wait and see”. The BJP has blamed Shiv Sena’s stubbornness for imposition of the President’s rule. In a joint press conference, the Congress and NCP said that the two parties would explore every possibility of forming a government together. Certain clarifications are to be discussed before joining hands, but the Governor was in no mood to give time. We need to understand that Shiv Sena did not share a common manifesto with NCP and Congress but the discussions have to take place to form a government.

Udhav Thackarey made it clear that he was not lured by either Congress or NCP to form a government. He said that forming a government is not a child’s play and for that we need time. The Governor has given us six months instead of 48 hours which we had requested and within this time a stable government maybe finally formed in Maharashtra otherwise the State would be once again forced upon with a midterm election.
