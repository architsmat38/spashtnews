How impactful is the corporate tax deduction going to be?

There are clear positives to the Diwali gift of the corporate tax cuts of the government. Before this announcement, industries with exemption paid more than 25% corporate tax. With a reduction in tax rate, our government hopes to be globally competitive. This tax cut will help in increasing earnings and thereby resulting in increased capital expenditure. The reduction in corporate tax rate to 15% for Greenfield manufacturing projects - set up on or after 01st October 2019 will further help escalate investments.

Indian economy is demand driven. To achieve an 8% GDP, India needs to spur demands. This can only happen by increasing the earnings of our countrymen. The fast moving economy was derailed due to demonetisation. The growth was on a regular decline every quarter. The implementation of a hurried GST added woes to the Indian economy during that time. The ill conceived economic consequences disrupted the life of Indians and spoiled the equilibrium of the nation. This all contributed to very low revenue earnings. The GST collections have not matched the expectations. Resources have dried up and has negatively impacted the implementation of social beneficial measures.

The PM should now persuade the US President Donald Trump to revise his decision to exclude India from Generalised System of Preferences List (GSP). In turn, the US President may seek tariff concessions on US products exported to India, especially medical devices and agricultural products. This may culminate into broad contours of a bilateral trade agreement.

The RBI Governor, Shaktikanta Das has said that reduction in corporate tax may result in making India a lucrative destination for Foreign Investments. He said the tax cut move is no doubt a very bold measure and has now become very attractive compared to (Association of SouthEast Asian Nations) ASEAN countries. Finance Minister, Nirmala Sitharaman, had on Friday slashed corporate tax rates worth 1.45 trillion rupees paid by domestic manufacturers, thus making the country one of the lowest tax regimes in Asia. It has now come down to 25.17% from 34.9% for the existing companies and for new companies from 29.12% to 17.01%. This will hopefully have a very positive impact on the declining economy.

However, the RBI Governor has been cautioned by the economists in cutting policy rate from now as the physical impact of the governmentâ€™s largesse for the corporate sector could further reduce revenue collection by 0.7% of GDP. But the measures taken by the government have resulted in a sudden boom in the stock market. It is good news for the corporate sector.

But the question is - Is it going to impact the common man at all? The government needs to address the real problems of consumption and demand which are confronting our Indian Economy. Had this money been given directly to the common man in the form of tax rebates the capital inflow would have directly benefited the consumer market, thus leading to increase in demand. It could have been a direct and proactive solution to increase the Indian economy. What we need now, are policy interventions for effecting structural changes in our skill development programs. This can only be achieved by a paradigm shift in imparting education. Like the saying goes - “an inept doctor can seldom prescribe the right medicine.”
