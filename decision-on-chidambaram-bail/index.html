Decision on Chidambaram’s interim bail soon

Photo: PTI

The latest on P Chidambaram is that the Supreme Court has asked the trial court to consider Chidambaram’s plea for an interim bail in the INX Media Case. The INX Media Case relates to alleged irregularities in the foreign investment clearance given to the media group to the tune of 305 crores in the year 2007 by the then Finance Minister, P Chidambaram. In the meanwhile, Chidambaram’s custody has been extended by one more day. The former Finance Minister requested not be sent to Tihar Jail under judicial custody and instead, made himself available for a house arrest. A bench of two judges R.Banumathi and A.S Bopanna directed the CBI for his point of view on Chidambaram’s plea where the trial court has challenged the non bailable warrant.

Senior Congress leader and an advocate to Chidambaram, Kabil Sibal has informed the bench that since he has been in the CBI custody for the last 12 days, he should now be granted interim bail by the trial court. Sibal said the orders of the trial court can be set aside by the Supreme Court in granting him the interim bail. The bench said that Chidambaram can appeal to the trial court for his interim bail and can seek appropriate appeal as per the law. In case of rejection by the trial court, Sibal has requested the apex court to give Chidambaram protection till the petition is decided upon.

The bench has decided to hear the interim bail plea on the 5th of September. But as of now, there seems to be no end to Chidambaram’s miseries.
