Great Victory - A historic record for India against South Africa

Photo: AFP

What a victory has it been for the “Men in White”. This is India’s biggest victory over South Africa by an innings and 137 runs. To add icing on the cake this is India’s eleventh consecutive home series win which is a new world record. Earlier it was in the name of Australia who had won ten straight home series.

The toss was won by the Indian team and Captain Virat Kohli decided to bat first. The tone of a big score was set up by Mayank Agarwal and Cheteshwar Pujara and was carried forward by Virat, Rahane and Jadeja. Mayank Agarwal scored back to back centuries (the first was a double ton in Visakhapatnam). Pujara added valuable runs with him in the morning session when the ball was swinging too much. As Rohit went early, Agarwal and Pujara steadied the innings. On the backdrop of Kohli’s double century score of 254 (not out), his highest in Test Cricket, and the highest double centuries (7) by an Indian and a blistering knock of 91 by Jadeja, helped India score a mammoth 600 runs which as Kohli said had not anticipated.

Kohli, Jadeja, Rahane all piled miseries on the South African bowlers especially Keshav Maharaj who went for plenty. When India finally declared, the South African team was reeling under the pressure of a huge score, and lost three quick wickets at stumps. The next day was no better, although the South African Captain Faf du Plessis and Wicketkeeper Quinton de Kock tried to steady the ship with a 75 run stand for the sixth wicket. Later Keshav Maharaj and Vernon Philander stitched a valuable 109 run stand for the ninth wicket. But with three balls left with the third day’s play, India wrapped up the South African innings at 275.

Photo: AFP

This gave the Indian Captain enough time to take a decision on enforcing the follow on and they did just that today morning. Ishant Sharma struck early and Wriddhiman Saha took a stunner on the leg side off Umesh Yadav to dismiss De Bruyn. Hardly any resistance has been shown by the South African batsmen in the ongoing series except for Dean Elgar and Quinton de Kock in the first test at Visakhapatnam.

This indeed is a historic moment for India as this is their biggest win over South Africa. India has so far given an all round display of quality bowling, batting and fielding. The trio of pace battery in absence of Jasprit Bumrah have been very effective and difficult to negotiate. The spin duo of Ravindra Jadeja and Ravi Chandran Ashwin have already cast a spell on the South African batsmen.

They really need to gather themselves otherwise they are heading for 3-0 whitewash. The third test begins on 19th October but in the meanwhile let the Indian team enjoy this historic moment. Well played India!!
