The Progressive Teacher : Importance of Teacher Student Relationship

GururBrahma GururVishnu Gururdevo Maheshwara GururSakshaat ParaBrahma Tasmay ShriGurvey Namah

Guru - Formed of two words Gu - Darkness and Ru - Remover, someone who destroys darkness in our lives. A teacher student relationship has been given utmost importance in the Indian tradition. A true teacher is like a lamp to the student who takes him out of darkness and guides him on the path of success. Quoting Swami Vivekananda - “Great teachers are the living Gods on this earth, whom else should we worship”. The three teachers in a student’s lives are - his parents, his school/college/tuition teachers, his spiritual guru. The three Gurus together help a student see what is right or wrong. The values granted by them become a child’s thoughts and the thoughts are indeed responsible for shaping his future.

However, in the present day scenario, this relationship is changing. It is not just one but many factors causing this change. There are a lot of suicide and molesation cases in schools and colleges. There are cases when teachers try to insult students without even thinking of how it can affect these young children. On the other side of the coin, the parents generally perceive teachers as someone who they pay, it is quite unpleasant at times. With new gadgets coming in, the trust in the teachers is lost and it needs to be regained. We must understand that true knowledge is not confined to a store of e-books. It is the love, the affection and the compassion that a teacher can impart to a student which inturn fills his life with light and happiness. Patience is the key here.

An effective teacher student relationship can maximise the learning output. The enthusiasm and passion increases five folds. A teacher is all in all for his student. He can be a soldier - when he catches his students doing something wrong, a philosopher - when he imparts knowledge to his students, a parent - when he looks after the students in schools with love and care, a friend - when his students confide in him. This takes a significant amount of time and effort, but the benefit on both student and teacher is indeterminable.
