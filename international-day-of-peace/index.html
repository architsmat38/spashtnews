International Day of Peace - Let’s make this world a place of peace

Photo: unmiss.unmissions.org

International Day of Peace is celebrated on 21st September every year. To inaugurate the day, the United Nations peace bell is rung at the United Nations Headquarters in New York. The bell is made from coins donated by children from all continents except Africa and was a gift from the United Nations Association of Japan as a reminder of the human cost of war. The inscription on its sides read “Long live absolute world peace”.

The UN Secretary General Antonio Guterres quotes on the UN website that “Today peace faces a new danger: the climate emergency, which threatens our security, our livelihoods and our lives. That is why it is in the focus of International Day of Peace. And that’s why I am convening a Climate Action Summit.” The Sustainable Development Goal 13: “Climate Action” is a call for immediate action by all, to lower greenhouse emissions, build resilience and improve education on climate change. The theme for this year is Climate Action for peace. According to the UN website, the UN calls upon to take action and tackle climate change. On 23rd September 2019, a meeting is being convened by the UN with a concrete plan to accelerate Paris Agreement.

India stands at 141 among 163 countries on the Global Peace Index 2019. Iceland remains the most peaceful country in the world, a position it has held since 2008. India together with the Philippines, Japan, Bangladesh, Myanmar, China, Indonesia, Vietnam and Pakistan are the nine countries with the highest risk of multiple climate hazards.

To quote the Dalai Lama XIV, “Peace is not just mere absence of violence. Peace is, I think, the manifestation of human compassion. World peace must develop from inner peace”. How can we help to maintain world peace. Charity begins at home. Go home and love your family and surroundings because that is where peace begins and ends. World Peace Day is envisioned to become a moment of global unity. It is upto each one of us to make this a reality. Darkness can not drive out darkness, only light can do that. Hate can not drive out hate, only love can do that.
