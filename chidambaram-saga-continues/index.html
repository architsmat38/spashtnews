The Chidambaram Saga Continues

The verdict has come. Former Finance Minister, P Chidambaram has been jailed for fourteen days of judicial custody. This is a major blow to former Finance Minister and his continuous efforts to evade jail. The verdict given by the Special Judge, Ajay Kumar Kuhar sent Chidambaram to prison till 19th September where he would be kept in a special cell because he had a Z category protection. Thus ruling comes after the twin bench comprising of R Bhanumathi and A. S. Bopanna denied him bail in the money laundering case.

The Supreme Court informed his lawyers that the probe may be influenced hence custodial interrogation was important. The court further said considering the stage of investigation bail can not be granted. The Enforcement Directorate (ED) has to be given a free hand for a comprehensive investigation of this case. However, the Delhi High Court was under the scanner for denying him anticipatory bail on the evidence produced by the ED. The defence lawyers made an all out effort to stop the special judge from sending Chidambaram to jail but it was futile. Senior lawyer Kapil Sibal said that there was no justification for sending him to prison. He further gave a plea that Chidambaram never tampered with any evidence and cooperative with the CBI team. The ED was ready to arrest him anytime after the verdict went in their favour but Judge Kuhar was in favour of sending him to jail considering the facts and circumstances of the case. The Chidambarams have denied all allegations against them in both the cases (INX Media and Aircel Maxis where P Chidamabram is the prime accused for violating the then foreign investment laws) and alleged that NDA is nursing a political vendetta against him and his son. But until we get clarity on this from ED, Chidambaram will have to spend time with the jail inmates. For him this is getting from bad to worse.
