Jio: Creating disruption in the telecom industry

Digital revolution is growing at an exponential pace in India. Chairman and Managing Director of Reliance Industries, Mukesh Ambani, left no stone unturned to be a part of such exponential growth. With the launch of Jio, 3 years ago, Reliance had unleashed the price war with telecom competitors by offering throwaway tariff plans to the users. At the 42nd Reliance India Limited Annual General Meeting, Mukesh Ambani announced that Jio has managed to cross 340 million subscribers mark and it is adding around 10 million new subscribers every month, which will enable them to hit 500 million subscribers mark very soon. With such a growth, Jio has become the largest telecom operator in India and 2nd largest telecom operator in the world (in a single country) in terms of revenue market share.

Even with the single engine of revenue, mobile broadband, Jio has been profitable from its first quarter of commercial operations. It is now ready to turn on the other 4 engines (i.e. Internet of things, Home broadband, Enterprise Broadband and SMB Broadband) to boost its revenue. Starting with the launch of Jio Fiber on 5th September 2019 (the 3rd year anniversary of Jio), it is ready to disrupt the India’s telecom sector by providing broadband with high speed connectivity at the starting price of ₹700/month going upto ₹10,000/month, which is 1/10th of the global rates. Jio Fiber will provide a speed of 100 Mbps and can go upto 1 Gbps. These tariff plans will also include features like free calling from fixed line and will provide the lowest international rates. It would also include unlimited international calling pack to US and Canada for just ₹500/month. Jio Fiber will act as a foundation for Jio set top box, enabling users to get 4k high definition media content at a nominal price. As a welcome offer, Jio has also announced that the customers with Jio Fiber will also get HD/4K television set and set top box for free, however this will only be applicable for the customers subscribed to Jio Forever plans (yearly plan).

Jio has completely reshaped the Indian telecom sector, leaving Vodafone Idea and Airtel as its competitors. Based on the reported losses of revenue and subscriber base, Vodafone Idea can unlikely be seen as a big competitor of Jio. However, Sunil Mittal’s Bharti Airtel is gearing up to compete with Jio, by growing its revenue and improving the quality of subscriber base. Airtel has started focussing on improving the quality rather than focussing on the quantity of its subscribers by introducing minimum recharge plans starting ₹35. It has resulted in the highest average revenue per user (ARPU) at ₹129, keeping Jio on the second position at ₹122. Earlier this year, Airtel had also launched a loyalty program for its prepaid users - Airtel Thanks. Postpaid customers are enjoying Netflix and Amazon Prime which is already included in their tariff plans. Airtel is trying to maintain the retention of its customers but it will be a tough fight with Jio having deep pockets. This competition will get intense since Jio is also entering into home services by buying majority stakes in cable TV companies Den Networks and Hathway Cable & Datacom, giving it access to 1100 cities and towns. It would be great to see how this telecom battle shapes itself in the upcoming years.
