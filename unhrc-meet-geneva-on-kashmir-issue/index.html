UNHRC meet at Geneva on Kashmir issue

Photo: AFP

A significant meeting is going to be held between India and Pakistan at Geneva, Switzerland today on the issue of Kashmir at United Nations Human Rights Council (UNHRC) after international pressure on India to ease the situation in Jammu and Kashmir.

Shah Muhammad Qureshi, Finance Minister of Pakistan would be representing his country at the session of UNHRC. His talks will focus on the present situation in Jammu and Kashmir following India’s decision to abrogate Article 370 and reorganise it into two Union Territories including Ladakh. The Indian point of view would be represented by a secretary from the External Affairs Ministry and Ajay Bisaria, the former High Commissioner to Islamabad. A Cabinet Minister has not been sent because India does not want to give much importance to Pakistan on Kashmir issue. Pakistan on the other hand, has created a hue and cry to attract International support on Kashmir issue, as it still considers Kashmir as an independent state with a special status. To counter this, India could raise the issue of violation of human rights in Pakistan and very recently the conversions of Hindus and Sikhs forcefully. India would voice its concern over Pakistan occupied Kashmir (PoK) where atrocities on the minorities have been witnessed.

India has played its cards well in reaching out to the 47 member countries of the UNHRC to get its backing on the Kashmir issue. A briefing session has been carried out in New Delhi for envoys and diplomats which includes members from Latin America, Africa, Asia and Europe. Diplomats from various countries accepted Kashmir as an internal matter of India with no interference from other countries. However, they also mentioned their concern about no communication and house arrests of influential politicians in the valley.

India will have to ease restrictions in Kashmir to make sure that they are able to garner support from UNHRC of which we are quite capable. This again will demoralise Pakistan who has been trying to raise this issue internationally ever since 5th August. As things will definitely ease out and restrictions lifted gradually, Pakistan may have no allies for support on the Kashmir issue.
