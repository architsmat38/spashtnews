RBI cuts repo rate by 25 basis points to 5.15%

The Reserve Bank of India (RBI) on Friday announced a decision to cut Key Policy Rate by 25 Basis Points to 5.15%. This will help stimulate consumer sentiments in the upcoming festive season, which augurs well for the real estate business. However, much depends on how the banks are going to pass on this benefit to the home loan buyers. The consumers are spending quite a bit less on FMCG products on automobiles and real estates. This may boost their confidence a little more on spending this festive season. This will definitely spur the real estate demand as more freebies will be served on a platter.

The property consultants are going all out to woo the home buyers with plenty on offer and have offered discounts upto 15-20%. With this cut on the Basis Points, the home buyers may explore opportunities to buy a home for themselves. But for the measures to reap results the banks need to extend this advantage to the consumer otherwise the entire exercise would prove to be abortive. If this is implemented then there might be an accelerated growth in the real estate sector this festive season.

But a certain trepidation has creeped in the market after a forecast of a weak Gross Domestic Product growth in the Fiscal Year 2019-20. Stock market is bleeding as it shed 770 points which is a clear low of 1.14%. Although the market was trading well, but with this forecast it plummeted. The forecast of 6.1% of growth is a clear departure from the growth anticipated for the Fiscal Year 2019-20 by the RBI which stood at 6.9%.

Despite the rate cut the market has reacted negatively, especially the banks. This would affect their profit ratio because they would have to pass on the benefit to the consumer. This means that they will have to compromise on the profit ratio. This statement comes from none other than Gaurav Dua, Senior Vice President and Head of Capital Market Strategy & Investment, Share Khan by BNP Paribas. RBI can not go any further with the cuts already provided this Fiscal Year which is the tune of 135 Basis Points. Top players of the Sensex Pack like Kotak Mahindra, Axis Bank, State Bank of India, L&T dropped to around 3.50% whereas TCS, Infosys, ONGC gained by 1.03%.

The RBI also announced a number of moves in the Payments space. The Central Bank announced that from December 2019 onwards National Electronic Fund Transfer (NEFT) will provide 24*7 services digitally. So far it is applicable on all working days between 8AM to 7PM, barring the second and the fourth Saturday. Given the rapid rise in the digital payment space, the RBI plans to publish more data covering payment system authorised by the Central Bank. But to boost consumer spending this repo rate cut may be an interim solution to give a flip to the real estate sector. This can only happen if the benefit of this is transmitted to the consumer.
