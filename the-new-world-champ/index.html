The New World Champ

Pusarla V Sindhu did the country proud by becoming the first Indian to win the coveted gold medal at the World Badminton Championship help at Basle, Switzerland. This was her third final appearance in this major tournament. Earlier she had to be content with back to back silver medals. Flash black takes me back to 2017 finals where she lost to Nozomi Okhura in a thrilling three setter. The scoreline read 21-19, 22-20, 22-20. She was again meeting her nemesis in the 2019 final. But this time she was better prepared.

The grit and the determination was more obvious this time and the hunger to win the title shown large on her face. This was going to be one of the crucial matches of her career. She had not won a single title in 2019 but this was going to be a different day. The scoreline of 21-7, 21-7 shows that she completely annihilated her opponent. What a sweet revenge it was. Okhura was absolutely mesmerised by her long corner shots and delectable drops on the net. She had no answer whatsoever. What we thought was going to be a thriller was converted by Sindhu as incredibly easy going on the court. I could see the moist eyes of this great athlete from India when the national anthem was being played for the first time in the World Badminton Championships.

This is no less a competition in comparison to the Olympics. Her Korean coach, Kim Ji Hyun has played a major part in tuning her rough edges and Pullela Gopichand was always as cool as a cucumber. What better gift could she give to her mother on her birthday. Sindhu get us that elusive Olympic goal at 2020 Tokyo. The entire nation is proud of you. Keep the spirit high, the country has great expectations from you in all the upcoming tournaments.
