The Kashmir Issue : A Chronological Description

The Kashmir dispute dates back to 1947. The Britishers before leaving India followed a ‘divide and rule’ policy on religious sentiments which led to the Partition of India. India had laid claim on the entire princely state of Jammu and Kashmir. Pakistan stakes claim on Kashmir due to its Muslim population and Chinese intervention on the Shaksgam Valley and Aksai Chin but it is primarily a conflict between India and Pakistan.

In 1947, immediately post partition Pakistani tribal military invaded Kashmir leaving the then Hindu ruler of Jammu and Kashmir, Maharaja Hari Singh, to shift his allegiance to India. The United Nations had to intervene to end the war and a Line of Control was drawn. This was followed by two more wars in 1965 and 1971. The 1971 war ended with a Simla Accord between the then Indian Prime Minister Smt Indira Gandhi and Pakistani Prime Minister Zulfiqar Ali Bhutto. It was decided upon that there would be no third party mediation on Kashmir issue. The Kashmir Issue gained momentum again in the year 1989 when the protest started and voices were being raised for an Independent Kashmir which was under the Indian government. The so called Kashmir Separatists were in a war like situation with the Indian government and were strongly supported and funded by the Pakistan government. Many terrorist groups came into formation and with support from the Pakistan Army became a nuisance in the entire Valley. This hurt the tourism a lot which was the mainstay of Kashmir economy. In 1999, India was once again at war in Kargil but gave Pakistan a befitting reply. Since 2010 several Separatist groups began pelting stones at security forces and disrupted government machinery in the Valley. The unrest erupted again in 2016 after the killing of Hizbul Mujahideen militant, Burhan Wani, by the Indian Security Forces. The militant outfit retaliated by a massive attack on the Indian Armed forces in Uri. This led to a major surgical strike by the Indian government. The Pulwama attack this year again forced the Indian government to undertake air strikes in which several militant outfit camps in Pakistan were destroyed. The final nail in the coffin is the abrogation of Article 370.

Pakistan has left no stone unturned to gather support internationally on this issue, the most recent one being the United Nations Human Rights Council (UNHRC) meet at Geneva. But again Pakistan had to come back empty handed as it was decided that this issue has to be decided bilaterally. Things are easing out gradually in Kashmir and we all hope that after lifting on sanctions, parity will be restored once again in the Kashmir Valley.
