Post US Summit, India’s relations with the other powerhouse countries of the world needs introspection

Photo: File

The past one week was one of the busiest schedules witnessed by the entire nation in respect to Prime Minister Narendra Modi’s US visit. After his speech at Houston, subsequent address to the United Nations General Assembly and Bloomberg Summit, India’s key message on Climate Change, Terrorism and Economic Strategies were on special mention. There were a few messages which clearly displayed India has a fast emerging powerhouse. India has connected its own development with a special emphasis on achievement of foreign policy goals. “Ease of timing” as PM Modi has always mentioned was a major factor discussed in several public interactions. The PM in his address gave a strong message that India was fast emerging as a modern economy with its relaxed policies on foreign investments.

India has improved its bilateral relations with the USA. PM Modi’s visit has further strengthened the political bond between the two leaders as was evident in “Howdy Modi” in Houston. Back home there was a feeling developing - there was some difference in the opinion of two leaders with respect to various political issues. This has been laid to rest for the time being. Even Washington has happily reciprocated to the visit of PM Modi.

But we need to understand that there is a sense of unease (to put it mildly) amongst the bigger powers. China is rising rapidly, there is an uncertain calm in Europe, the Gulf is volatile, Russia has its own stand on various international issues and very little can be said about Japan. It is here that a strong foreign policy of India will have to make sure that relations with these countries are also very cordial. Russia and Japan have always been close allies of India. But with Russia the undercurrents are not very positive. There has hardly been any statement issued by the Russian President Vladimir Putin on the Kashmir Issue and has always maintained that this needs to sorted bilaterally. France understands that India is a huge market for defence deals with Rafale at the helm of affairs. This will bring the two countries very close because of its trade value.

The only nation we can not really trust is China because of its close proximity to Pakistan. China and India can never be true friends, but at the same time they can not afford to be foes either. PM Modi on the back of strong mandate should make these countries his strong allies, bat with liberal but a strong foreign policy. It should not only be about the USA. We all know that engagements with China will continue at the border. But at the same time, we must assure that a competitive element can not be glossed over. Even China understands the bonhomie of New Delhi with Washington.

India will not have to focus on the declining economy in the coming months. India will have to deepen relations with the other powerhouses including those who have a difference with the USA. Only then we will be recognised as a country with multiple friends.
