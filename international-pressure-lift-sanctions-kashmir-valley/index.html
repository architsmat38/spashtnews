International Pressure on India to lift sanctions in Kashmir Valley : Action and not words required

Photo: AP

Anantnag district witnessed a grenade attack that has left fourteen seriously injured. The grenade attack was carried out by the suspected militants yesterday near the Deputy Commissioner of Police (DCP) office. As the news comes all the injured are stable. A traffic policeman was also injured. The intensity of the grenade was not much otherwise it would have left many casualties.

There has been a sporadic rise in the violent activities lately in the Valley. Earlier an ex Peoples Democratic Party worker, Sajad Hussain Manto was also injured in militant firing in Anantnag. He was shot in the abdomen and was rushed to the civil hospital where his condition is stated to be stable.

What is happening in the Valley should be a matter of serious concern. To ease matters, the Jammu and Kashmir Governor, Satyapal Malik, has allowed a delegation of National Conference from Jammu to meet its party President Farooq Abdullah and his son Omar Abdullah, currently under detention. This will be the first meeting of the National Conference leaders with the two former Chief Ministers after the abrogation of Article 370.

Meanwhile, as earlier mentioned during Prime Minister Narendra Modi’s visit to the United States that there was a certain disquiet amongst the Democrats in the US on the Kashmir issue. This is becoming more prominent now because the Democratic candidate for the Presidential Elections, Elizabeth Warren raised her concern on the ongoing restrictions and sanctions in Kashmir region. This concern has been raised by the candidate ahead of a Congressional hearing on 22nd October regarding Kashmir. The current President Donald Trump had earlier offered to mediate on this issue but later backtracked to let India and Pakistan solve it bilaterally. Senator Warren - a leading candidate for the party’s nomination tweeted her concern on the restrictions and blackout of communication. She further tweeted that India’s action is unacceptable and the sanctions must be lifted immediately.

Photo: Reuters

It is not only the US opposition but concerns have been raised by China, Malaysia and Turkey as well. Beijing’s allegiance to Pakistan on Kashmir is a major speed breaker in Indo China ties. China has always been a close ally of Pakistan and has always supported its cross border militant activities. Although, India has a border dispute with China over the last fifty years but they have always resolved the issue amicably and not a single bullet has been fired since the Nathula skirmish in 1967. On the backdrop of the Kashmir issue, Beijing shall be hosting Pakistan Prime Minister Imran Khan from 08th - 10th October. India must keep a close vigil on the bilateral meet because the only beneficiary of China at loggerheads with India is going to be Pakistan.

The Malaysian Prime Minister, Mahathir Mohamad, stated that India should resolve issues through negotiations rather than by invasions. Turkish President, Recep Tayyip Erdoğan also raised the Kashmir issue during his address at United Nations General Assembly, saying that “it is imperative to solve the problem through dialogue on the basis of justice, equity and not through collision”.

With the growing concerns internationally on the Kashmir issue the government must take measures that will be positive in lifting of the sanctions in Kashmir and restoring life to normalcy. The Indian government must take measures to lift restrictions, get the internet started and show the world that Kashmir is back to normal and definitely at peace.
